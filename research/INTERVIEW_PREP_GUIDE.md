# Interview Preparation Guide

## Common Interview Questions

### Tell Me About Yourself
**Structure:** Past → Present → Future
- Past: Where you're from, education background
- Present: What you're doing now (school, relevant experience)
- Future: Why this role/company, career goals

**Example:**
"I'm Miguel, a sophomore at [School] studying Finance and Computer Science. I'm a first-generation college student passionate about using technology to solve financial problems. 

Currently, I'm [relevant experience: coursework, projects, etc.]. I'm excited about this internship because [specific reason related to company/role].

Long-term, I want to [career goal that aligns with company]."

### Why This Company?
**Research Required:**
- Recent company news
- Company values/mission
- Specific programs/products
- Culture/Diversity initiatives

**Example:**
"I'm drawn to [Company] because of [specific reason]. I was especially impressed by [recent news/initiative]. 

As a [diversity background if applicable], I appreciate your commitment to [specific diversity program]. 

I want to work here because [specific role-related reason]."

### Why Finance + CS?
**Your Story:**
- What sparked interest in both?
- How do they complement each other?
- Specific examples of intersection

**Example:**
"I chose Finance and CS because I see technology transforming the financial industry. 

With CS, I can build tools to analyze data more efficiently. With Finance, I understand what problems need solving.

For example, [specific project/experience that combines both]."

### Tell Me About a Challenge
**STAR Method:**
- **S**ituation: Context
- **T**ask: What you needed to do
- **A**ction: What YOU did specifically
- **R**esult: Quantified outcome

**Example (First-Gen Student):**
"As a first-generation college student, I faced the challenge of navigating college admissions and financial aid without family guidance.

I had to figure out the FAFSA, scholarships, and course selection on my own.

I tackled this by [specific actions: meeting with advisors, research, etc.].

As a result, I [outcome: got into target school, secured funding, etc.]."

### Behavioral Questions

**Leadership:**
"Tell me about a time you led a team."
- Even small projects count
- Focus on YOUR specific contributions
- Quantify results

**Failure:**
"Tell me about a time you failed."
- Choose a real failure (not humble-brag)
- Focus on what you learned
- Show growth

**Teamwork:**
"Tell me about a time you worked with a difficult teammate."
- Stay professional
- Focus on resolution, not drama
- Emphasize communication

## Technical Questions (CS Roles)

### Data Structures & Algorithms
- Array manipulation
- String processing
- Hash maps/dictionaries
- Basic tree/graph traversal

### Preparation Resources
- LeetCode (Easy/Medium)
- HackerRank
- Pramp (mock interviews)
- Cracking the Coding Interview

### System Design (Junior Roles)
- Basic understanding
- Focus on trade-offs
- Think out loud

## Technical Questions (Finance Roles)

### Financial Concepts
- Three financial statements
- Valuation methods (DCF, comps)
- Market trends
- Basic accounting

### Market Questions
- "What stocks do you like?"
- "Recent market news?"
- "Walk me through a DCF"

### Case Studies
- Practice mental math
- Structure your thinking
- Ask clarifying questions

## Questions to Ask Them

### About the Role:
- "What does a typical day look like?"
- "What skills are you looking for in an ideal candidate?"
- "What projects would I work on?"

### About the Team:
- "How is the team structured?"
- "What's the culture like?"
- "How do you support intern development?"

### About Growth:
- "What do successful interns go on to do?"
- "What learning opportunities are available?"
- "How does the full-time conversion process work?"

## Post-Interview

### Within 24 Hours:
- Send thank-you email
- Reference specific conversation points
- Reiterate interest
- Include any additional materials promised

### If Rejected:
- Thank them for the opportunity
- Ask for feedback (optional)
- Stay connected on LinkedIn
- Apply again next cycle

### If Offered:
- Negotiate (even for internships, ask about start dates/projects)
- Get offer in writing
- Ask about next steps/onboarding
- Accept professionally and enthusiastically
